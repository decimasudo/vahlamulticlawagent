2. **Cultural contextualization** â€” Situate music within its social environment
   - What community or tradition does this music serve?

   - What social identities are being constructed or reinforced?

3. **Historical placement** â€” Understand musical development through time
   - How does this reflect or respond to technological innovations?
   - What earlier traditions does this build upon or reject?
   - How do social and political changes influence musical style?

4. **Function and meaning** â€” Analyze music's social and emotional work
   - What is this music trying to accomplish for its participants?
   - How does it create community, express identity, or mark important moments?
   - What values and worldviews are embedded in musical choices?

## Key Questions to Ask
1. How do the musical elements (rhythm, melody, harmony, timbre) work together to create specific effects?
2. What social or cultural work is this music doing for its community of users?
3. How does this music relate to the historical traditions and contemporary innovations of its time?
4. What does the way this music is performed, distributed, or consumed tell us about the society that created it?
5. How might different listeners (based on their cultural background and experience) hear this music differently?

## Vocabulary
| Term | Plain English |
|------|---------------|
| Timbre | The quality or "color" of a sound that distinguishes different instruments or voices |
| Polyphony | Multiple independent melodic lines sounding simultaneously |
| Genre | A category of music with shared conventions (jazz, hip-hop, symphony, etc.) |
| Vernacular | Informal, everyday musical practices within a community |
| Ethnomusicology | The study of music in its cultural context, especially non-Western traditions |

## When to Apply
- Analyzing how music is used in media, advertising, or political contexts
- Understanding cultural conflicts over musical style, authenticity, or appropriation
- Evaluating claims about musical "progress," decline, or universal aesthetics
- Interpreting the role of music in identity formation and social movements
