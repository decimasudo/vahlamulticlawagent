## Key Questions to Ask
1. How do the work's formal choices (style, structure, language) support its themes?
2. What does this text reveal about the human condition or the society that produced it?
3. How does this work position itself in relation to literary traditions and conventions?
4. What multiple ways can this text be reasonably interpreted, and what evidence supports each reading?
5. What makes this work artistically successful or significant beyond its immediate content?

|------|---------------|

| Irony | Gap between expectation and reality, or between stated and intended meaning |

| Unreliable narrator | Storyteller whose account we have reason to question or doubt |

## When to Apply
- Analyzing any written text for deeper meaning and persuasive techniques
- Understanding how storytelling and rhetoric work in media, politics, and culture
- Evaluating the artistic merit and cultural significance of creative works
- Developing more sophisticated reading and communication skills
