## Key Questions to Ask
1. What visual strategies does this work use to communicate its message or create its effect?
2. How would the original audience have understood and used this object?
3. What can this work tell us about the society, beliefs, and power structures of its time?
4. How does this work relate to artistic traditions and innovations of its period?
5. What has been included, excluded, or emphasized, and what does this reveal about the creator's intentions or constraints?

## When to Apply
- Analyzing visual media, advertising, or propaganda for hidden messages and techniques
- Understanding how images shape cultural values and social attitudes
- Evaluating claims about artistic authenticity, value, or historical significance
- Interpreting the visual culture and material environment of different societies
