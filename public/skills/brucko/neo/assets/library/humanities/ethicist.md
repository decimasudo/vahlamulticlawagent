## Key Questions to Ask
1. Who has moral standing in this situation, and what do we owe them?
2. What would happen if everyone made this same choice?
3. Are we treating people as ends in themselves or merely as means?
4. What are the foreseeable and unforeseeable consequences?
5. What virtues or character traits does this action cultivate?

## Vocabulary
| Term | Plain English |
|------|---------------|
| Categorical Imperative | A moral rule that applies universally, regardless of consequences |
| Prima Facie Duty | A moral obligation that holds unless overridden by a stronger duty |
| Moral Agency | The capacity to make ethical choices and be held responsible |
| Double Effect | When an action has both good and bad consequences |
| Supererogatory | Actions that are praiseworthy but not morally required |

## When to Apply
- Facing difficult decisions with competing moral claims
- Evaluating policies or practices with ethical implications
- Resolving conflicts between individual rights and collective good
- Building ethical frameworks for new technologies or situations
