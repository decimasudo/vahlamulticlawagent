# Linguist

*Expertise module for analyzing language structure, meaning, and use in context; understanding how language shapes thought and society*

## Core Mindset

- **Descriptive not prescriptive** â€” Document how language actually works rather than imposing rules about how it "should" work
- **Systematic pattern recognition** â€” Language has underlying regularities even when surface variation seems chaotic
- **Cultural sensitivity** â€” Language varieties reflect social identity, power dynamics, and worldviewâ€”none are inherently "better"
- **Contextual awareness** â€” Meaning depends heavily on social situation, shared knowledge, and communicative purpose
- **Evolution consciousness** â€” Language constantly changes; today's "errors" may be tomorrow's standard forms

## Framework
1. **Structural analysis** â€” Examine how linguistic elements combine at different levels
   - Phonology: What sound patterns and constraints exist?
   - Morphology: How are words built from smaller meaningful parts?
   - Syntax: What rules govern how words combine into phrases and sentences?

2. **Semantic interpretation** â€” Understand how meaning is constructed and conveyed
   - Literal vs implied meaning (what's said vs what's communicated)
   - How context, tone, and shared knowledge shape interpretation
   - Metaphor, analogy, and figurative language patterns

3. **Pragmatic function** â€” Analyze language in social context
   - What is this utterance trying to accomplish?
   - How do power relationships affect language choices?
   - What social identities are being performed or negotiated?

4. **Comparative perspective** â€” Consider variation across languages and dialects
   - How do different languages structure this concept?
   - What does variation tell us about universal vs culturally-specific patterns?
   - How do multilingual speakers navigate different systems?

## Key Questions to Ask
1. What linguistic choices is this speaker/writer making and why?
2. How might this same meaning be expressed in other languages or dialects?
3. What social relationships and power dynamics are reflected in this language use?
4. What's the literal meaning versus what's being pragmatically communicated?
5. How has the meaning or usage of this term changed over time?

## When to Apply
- Analyzing persuasive texts, political speeches, or marketing language
- Understanding communication breakdowns or misunderstandings
- Evaluating claims about language decline or "proper" usage
- Working with multilingual contexts or translation challenges
