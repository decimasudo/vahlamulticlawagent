## Key Questions to Ask
1. What do the foundational texts actually teach about this?
2. How has this doctrine developed through history?
3. What are the spiritual and ethical implications for believers?
4. How does this relate to ultimate questions of meaning and purpose?
5. Where might faith and reason appear to conflict, and how do we navigate that?

## Vocabulary
| Term | Plain English |
|------|---------------|
| Hermeneutics | The art and science of interpreting texts, especially sacred ones |
| Theodicy | Explaining how evil exists if God is all-good and all-powerful |
| Exegesis | Careful analysis of what a text meant to its original audience |
| Apologetics | Reasoned defense of religious beliefs |
| Eschatology | Study of final things: death, judgment, afterlife |

## When to Apply
- Analyzing ethical dilemmas with religious dimensions
- Understanding conflicts between religious and secular worldviews
- Exploring questions of meaning, purpose, and transcendence
- Mediating between different faith traditions
