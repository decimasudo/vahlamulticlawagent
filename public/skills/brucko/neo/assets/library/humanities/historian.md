*Expertise module for analyzing past events, understanding cause-and-effect across time, and contextualizing present situations through historical perspective*

## Framework
1. **Source evaluation** â€” Assess primary vs secondary sources, check for bias, authenticate documents
   - Who created this source and why?
   - What perspective or agenda might influence their account?
   - How close in time/space were they to the events?

2. **Contextual mapping** â€” Place events within their broader social, economic, and political environment
   - What were the dominant ideas/technologies/power structures of this era?
   - How did geography, climate, and resources shape possibilities?

3. **Causal tracing** â€” Identify immediate triggers vs underlying causes
   - What were the short-term precipitating events?
   - What long-term trends or tensions made this outcome likely?
   - How did contingent factors (individual decisions, chance events) alter the course?

4. **Comparative analysis** â€” Draw parallels and contrasts with similar situations
   - Has this pattern occurred before in other times/places?
   - What made outcomes similar or different?
   - What lessons can be extracted while respecting unique circumstances?

## Key Questions to Ask
1. What primary sources exist for this claim, and what are their limitations?
2. How did people living through this actually experience and understand these events?
3. What alternative outcomes were possible given the constraints and knowledge of the time?
4. Which voices and perspectives have been marginalized or lost in traditional accounts?
5. How do short-term political events relate to longer-term social and economic trends?

## When to Apply
- Analyzing current political or social crises that have historical precedents
- Evaluating claims about "unprecedented" events or "lessons of history"
- Understanding the roots of ongoing conflicts or institutional arrangements
- Assessing the reliability of narratives about past events that shape current decisions
