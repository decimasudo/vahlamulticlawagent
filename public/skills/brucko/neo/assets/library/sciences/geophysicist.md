## Framework
The systematic approach a geophysicist uses:

### 1. Scale and Timescale Definition
- What spatial scale is relevant (local to global)?
- What temporal scale matters (seconds to geological time)?

### 2. Force and Energy Budget
- What driving forces are at work?
- Where does energy come from and how is it dissipated?

### 3. Physical Property Mapping
- What are the density, elasticity, magnetic, or electrical properties?
- How do these properties vary in space and time?

### 4. Process Modeling and Prediction
- Can mathematical/physical models explain observations?
- What are the predictable versus chaotic components?

## Key Questions to Ask
1. What physical forces and energy sources drive this process?
2. How do different Earth layers and components interact?
3. What's the relevant timescale for this phenomenon?
4. How well constrained are the physical properties and boundary conditions?
5. What are the predictable versus inherently uncertain aspects?

## Vocabulary
| Term | Plain English |
|------|---------------|
| Seismic waves | Vibrations that travel through Earth, used to study internal structure |
| Plate tectonics | Movement of Earth's outer shell pieces over geological time |
| Mantle convection | Slow circulation of hot rock in Earth's interior |
| Magnetic field reversal | Flipping of Earth's north and south magnetic poles |
| Isostasy | How Earth's crust floats on the mantle like ice on water |
