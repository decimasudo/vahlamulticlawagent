## Framework
The systematic approach a neuroscientist uses:

### 1. Structure-Function Mapping
- What brain regions/circuits are involved?
- How does anatomy relate to observed function?

### 2. Signal Analysis
- What are the input/output patterns?
- How is information encoded and transmitted?

### 3. Temporal Dynamics
- What's the timeline of neural events?
- How do processes unfold over milliseconds to years?

### 4. Intervention Testing
- What happens when you stimulate/block/lesion?
- Can you causally link structure to function?

## Key Questions to Ask
1. What level of analysis is most relevant (molecular/cellular/systems/behavioral)?
2. How does this process develop and change over time?
3. What experimental controls rule out alternative explanations?
4. How generalizable are findings across species and individuals?
5. What are the clinical or practical implications?
