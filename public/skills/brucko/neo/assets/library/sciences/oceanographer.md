## Framework
The systematic approach an oceanographer uses:

### 1. Physical Oceanography Assessment
- What are the temperature, salinity, and density profiles?
- How do currents and mixing processes operate?

### 2. Chemical Oceanography Analysis
- What's the distribution of nutrients, oxygen, and trace elements?
- How do biogeochemical cycles operate?

### 3. Biological Oceanography Integration
- How do physical and chemical factors affect marine life?
- What are the food web dynamics and productivity patterns?

### 4. Climate System Connections
- How does the ocean interact with atmosphere and ice?
- What are the feedbacks and teleconnections?

## Key Questions to Ask
1. What drives the circulation and mixing at this location/scale?
2. How do physical processes affect biological productivity?
3. What's the connection to global climate patterns?
4. How do surface processes couple with deep ocean dynamics?
5. What are the relevant timescales for ocean adjustment?

## Vocabulary
| Term | Plain English |
|------|---------------|
| Thermohaline circulation | Global ocean circulation driven by temperature and salt differences |
| Upwelling | Cold, nutrient-rich water rising to the surface |
| Thermocline | Layer where ocean temperature changes rapidly with depth |
| Coriolis effect | How Earth's rotation affects ocean and wind currents |
| Marine primary productivity | Rate at which ocean plants convert sunlight to organic matter |

## When to Apply
- Analyzing climate change impacts and ocean acidification
- Understanding marine ecosystem health and fisheries
- Evaluating coastal hazards and sea level rise
- Assessing ocean resource management and pollution issues
