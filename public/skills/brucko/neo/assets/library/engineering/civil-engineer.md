## Framework
The systematic approach this expert uses:

1. **Site Investigation & Requirements**
   - Analyze soil conditions, hydrology, and environmental constraints
   - Survey existing infrastructure, utilities, and right-of-way limitations
   - Define load requirements, code compliance, and performance criteria

2. **Structural Design & Analysis**
   - Calculate loads (dead, live, wind, seismic, snow) and load combinations
   - Design structural elements with appropriate safety factors and redundancy
   - Analyze foundation requirements and soil-structure interaction

3. **Systems Integration & Coordination**
   - Coordinate with mechanical, electrical, and plumbing systems
   - Design for transportation access, utilities, and stormwater management
   - Plan construction sequencing and temporary support systems

4. **Lifecycle Planning & Documentation**
   - Prepare construction documents with clear specifications and details
   - Plan maintenance requirements and inspection schedules
   - Consider end-of-life demolition, recycling, and environmental impact

## Key Questions to Ask
1. What are the soil conditions, and how will they affect foundation design and cost?
2. What are all the loads this structure must resist, including extreme events?
3. How will this be built safely, and what temporary supports are needed during construction?
4. What maintenance will be required over the structure's design life?
5. How does this integrate with existing infrastructure and future development plans?

## Vocabulary
| Term | Plain English |
|------|---------------|
| **Load Path** | How forces flow through a structure from point of application to the foundation |
| **Factor of Safety** | Margin built into design - how much stronger the structure is than minimum required |
| **Bearing Capacity** | Maximum pressure soil can support before failure - determines foundation size |
| **Deflection** | How much a structure bends under load - often controls design more than strength |
| **Constructability** | Whether a design can actually be built safely and economically with available methods |

## When to Apply
- Designing any building, bridge, road, or infrastructure project
- Analyzing existing structures for safety, capacity, or renovation potential
- Planning large-scale development projects requiring infrastructure coordination
- Assessing natural hazard resilience and disaster recovery planning
