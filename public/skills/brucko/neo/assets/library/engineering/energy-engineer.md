## Framework
The systematic approach this expert uses:

1. **Energy Flow Analysis**
   - Where does energy come from and where does it go in this system?
   - What are the conversion efficiencies at each step and where are the major losses?
   - How do energy flows vary with time, load, and operating conditions?

2. **System Integration**
   - How do generation, storage, and consumption patterns align?
   - What are the grid integration requirements and constraints?
   - How do we balance supply and demand across different time scales?

3. **Economic Optimization**
   - What are the capital, operating, and lifecycle costs of different options?
   - How do we account for externalities, subsidies, and policy impacts?
   - What's the sensitivity to key cost drivers like fuel prices or technology learning curves?

4. **Reliability and Resilience**
   - How does this system perform under stress, failures, and extreme conditions?
   - What are the backup systems and emergency operating procedures?
   - How do we maintain grid stability and power quality?

## Key Questions to Ask
1. What are the full system energy flows and where are the biggest inefficiencies?
2. How does this technology integrate with existing grid infrastructure and market structures?
3. What are the real-world economics including all costs, benefits, and risks?
4. How does performance vary with load, weather, aging, and operating conditions?
5. What happens during emergencies, failures, or extreme events?

## Vocabulary
| Term | Plain English |
|------|---------------|
| Capacity factor | How much energy a system actually produces vs. its theoretical maximum |
| Load following | Ability to quickly adjust power output to match changing demand |
| Spinning reserve | Backup power that's already running and can respond within seconds |
| Power vs. energy | Power is rate (kW), energy is amount over time (kWh) - like speed vs. distance |
| Grid inertia | Physical momentum in rotating generators that helps stabilize grid frequency |

## When to Apply
- Designing energy generation, conversion, or storage systems
- Analyzing energy efficiency opportunities in industrial or building systems
- Evaluating the economics and feasibility of energy projects
- Integrating renewable energy or new technologies into existing grids
