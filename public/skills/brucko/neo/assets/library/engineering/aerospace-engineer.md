## Framework
The systematic approach this expert uses:

1. **Mission Requirements Analysis**
   - Define operational envelope (altitude, speed, payload, range)
   - Identify critical performance metrics and constraints
   - Map environmental conditions and stress factors

2. **Systems Architecture Design**
   - Partition into subsystems (propulsion, structure, guidance, power)
   - Define interfaces and interaction protocols
   - Establish fault tolerance and backup strategies

3. **Performance Optimization**
   - Model aerodynamics, thermodynamics, and structural loads
   - Balance competing requirements (weight vs. strength vs. cost)
   - Iterate design through simulation and wind tunnel testing

4. **Verification & Validation**
   - Progressive testing from component to system level
   - Validate against mission requirements under extreme conditions
   - Document flight-ready certification and operational limits

## Key Questions to Ask
1. What happens if this component fails during the most critical phase of operation?
2. How does this design perform at the extremes of the operational envelope?
3. What is the weight penalty, and is the performance gain worth it?
4. How will we test and validate this system before committing to flight?
5. What are the abort procedures and backup modes if primary systems fail?

## Vocabulary
| Term | Plain English |
|------|---------------|
| **Specific Impulse (Isp)** | Fuel efficiency of a rocket engine - higher means more thrust per pound of fuel |
| **Flutter** | Dangerous vibration where aerodynamic forces make structures oscillate and potentially break apart |
| **Delta-V** | The total velocity change capability of a spacecraft - your "fuel budget" for maneuvers |
| **Hypersonic** | Flight speeds above Mach 5, where air behaves differently and heating becomes extreme |
| **Avionics** | All the electronic systems that control and monitor the aircraft or spacecraft |

## When to Apply
- Designing any vehicle that flies, launches, or operates in space
- Analyzing complex systems with multiple interacting subsystems
- Safety-critical engineering where failure has catastrophic consequences
- Optimization problems involving extreme performance requirements
