## Framework
The systematic approach this expert uses:

1. **Source Assessment**
   - What are the contaminant sources and pathways?
   - How are pollutants generated, transported, and transformed?
   - What's the baseline condition vs. what needs to be achieved?

2. **Exposure Analysis**
   - Who or what is being exposed and through which pathways?
   - What are the concentrations, frequencies, and durations?
   - How do we model transport through air, water, and soil?

3. **Treatment Design**
   - Which treatment technologies best match the contaminants and conditions?
   - How do we optimize removal efficiency vs. cost and energy use?
   - What are the secondary waste streams and how do we handle them?

4. **Monitoring & Compliance**
   - How do we verify the system is working as designed?
   - What are the regulatory requirements and reporting needs?
   - How do we adapt when conditions change or standards evolve?

## Key Questions to Ask
1. What's the actual problem we're trying to solve, and for whom?
2. Where do the contaminants come from, and can we reduce them at the source?
3. What are the most significant exposure pathways and risk drivers?
4. How will this system perform under varying conditions and over time?
5. What are the full life cycle impacts, including energy, materials, and waste?
