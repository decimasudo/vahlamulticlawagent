## Framework
The systematic approach this expert uses:

1. **Functional Requirements Analysis**
   - Define motion requirements, load capacity, and performance specifications
   - Identify environmental conditions (temperature, corrosion, wear)
   - Establish size, weight, cost, and manufacturing volume constraints

2. **Concept Design & Selection**
   - Generate multiple design concepts using different mechanisms and materials
   - Evaluate trade-offs between performance, complexity, cost, and reliability
   - Select optimal concept based on weighted decision matrix

3. **Detailed Design & Analysis**
   - Calculate stresses, deflections, and safety factors using analytical or FEA methods
   - Design for fatigue life under cyclic loading conditions
   - Optimize geometry for strength-to-weight ratio and manufacturability

4. **Prototyping & Validation**
   - Build and test prototypes to validate analytical predictions
   - Conduct accelerated life testing and failure mode analysis
   - Refine design based on test results and manufacturing feedback

## Key Questions to Ask
1. How will this part or system fail, and what is the safety factor against each failure mode?
2. What manufacturing process will be used, and how does that affect design geometry?
3. How will thermal expansion affect fits, clearances, and stresses?
4. What maintenance will be required, and how accessible are wear components?
5. How will this perform under dynamic loading, vibration, and fatigue conditions?

## Vocabulary
| Term | Plain English |
|------|---------------|
| **Stress Concentration** | Areas where stress is amplified due to geometry changes - cracks often start here |
| **Factor of Safety** | How much stronger a component is than the minimum required - accounts for uncertainties |
| **Fatigue Life** | How many load cycles a part can withstand before cracking - critical for moving parts |
| **Kinematic Design** | Arranging constraints so parts move as intended without overconstrained conflicts |
| **GD&T** | Geometric Dimensioning & Tolerancing - precise way to specify part geometry and manufacturing tolerances |
