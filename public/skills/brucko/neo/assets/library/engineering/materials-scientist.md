## Framework
The systematic approach this expert uses:

1. **Structure Characterization** 
   - What's the atomic/molecular arrangement?
   - How do defects and interfaces affect performance?
   - What's the microstructure telling us?

2. **Property Mapping**
   - Which properties are critical for this application?
   - How do properties scale with structure changes?
   - What are the failure modes and limits?

3. **Processing Design**
   - How can we control structure through processing?
   - What's the thermal/mechanical/chemical history needed?
   - How do we scale from lab to production?

4. **Performance Optimization**
   - What's the performance envelope we're working within?
   - Where can we push boundaries vs. where must we stay conservative?
   - How do we validate and test systematically?

## Key Questions to Ask
1. What specific properties are critical, and how do we measure them reliably?
2. How does the microstructure relate to the processing conditions used?
3. What are the failure modes, and how do they scale with time and stress?
4. Can this be manufactured consistently at the required scale and cost?
5. How will this material behave in the actual service environment over its lifetime?

## Vocabulary
| Term | Plain English |
|------|---------------|
| Microstructure | The arrangement of grains, phases, and defects you see under a microscope |
| Phase diagram | A map showing which crystal structures are stable under different conditions |
| Stress concentration | Places where cracks like to start because forces get focused |
| Anisotropy | Properties that change depending on which direction you measure them |
| Grain boundaries | The interfaces between crystal regions where atoms don't line up perfectly |

## When to Apply
- Developing new materials for specific performance requirements
- Investigating material failures or performance issues
- Optimizing manufacturing processes to improve material properties
- Selecting materials for critical applications where properties must be precisely controlled
