## Framework
The systematic approach this expert uses:

1. **Process Flow Development**
   - Map the complete chemical transformation from raw materials to products
   - Identify all inputs, outputs, byproducts, and waste streams
   - Design separation and purification steps to achieve target specifications

2. **Mass & Energy Balance Analysis**
   - Calculate material flows through each unit operation
   - Analyze heat integration opportunities and energy recovery
   - Optimize reactor conditions (temperature, pressure, residence time, catalysts)

3. **Equipment Design & Selection**
   - Size reactors, separators, heat exchangers, and pumps based on process requirements
   - Consider materials of construction for corrosion resistance and safety
   - Design control systems for stable, automated operation

4. **Economic & Safety Assessment**
   - Estimate capital and operating costs for the entire process
   - Conduct hazard analysis and implement safety management systems
   - Optimize for profitability while meeting environmental and safety regulations

## Key Questions to Ask
1. What are the fundamental mass and energy balances, and where are the bottlenecks?
2. How will this process behavior change when scaled from lab to industrial production?
3. What safety hazards exist, and how are they controlled through design rather than procedures?
4. What is the total cost of ownership, including raw materials, energy, waste disposal, and maintenance?
5. How sensitive is process performance to variations in feed quality, operating conditions, and catalyst activity?

## Vocabulary
| Term | Plain English |
|------|---------------|
| **Unit Operation** | Individual processing steps (distillation, reaction, filtration) that can be analyzed and designed separately |
| **Residence Time** | How long material stays in a reactor or processing unit - critical for conversion and product quality |
| **Heat Integration** | Using hot streams to heat cold streams, recovering energy and reducing utility costs |
| **Selectivity** | Fraction of reactant converted to desired product vs. unwanted byproducts |
| **HAZOP** | Hazard and Operability Study - systematic method to identify process safety risks |

## When to Apply
- Designing manufacturing processes involving chemical reactions or separations
- Optimizing existing industrial processes for efficiency or cost reduction
- Scaling laboratory processes to pilot plant or commercial production
- Analyzing any system involving mass and energy flows
