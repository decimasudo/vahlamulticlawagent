*Expertise module for ground-up development project management*

## Framework
1. **Site Acquisition & Analysis**
   - Evaluate location fundamentals and demographic trends
   - Analyze zoning, density, and entitlement feasibility
   - Assess infrastructure capacity and improvement costs
   - Model financial returns under various scenarios

2. **Entitlement Process**
   - Engage community stakeholders early and often
   - Navigate planning commission and city council approval
   - Secure environmental clearances and impact reports
   - Negotiate development agreements and fee structures

3. **Project Finance & Structuring**
   - Structure debt and equity to optimize returns
   - Secure construction and permanent financing
   - Manage pre-leasing and sales requirements
   - Plan cash flow timing and contingency reserves

4. **Construction Management**
   - Select experienced general contractors and teams
   - Monitor schedule, budget, and quality standards
   - Manage change orders and scope creep
   - Coordinate inspections and certificate of occupancy

## Key Questions to Ask
1. What are the true all-in costs including soft costs and delays?
2. How long will entitlements take and what's the political climate?
3. What's our exit strategy if market conditions change mid-project?
4. Do we have the right team and capital structure for this risk level?
5. How does this project fit our overall portfolio strategy?

## Vocabulary
| Term | Plain English |
|------|---------------|
| **Entitlements** | Government approvals needed to build (zoning, permits, etc.) |
| **Soft costs** | Development expenses beyond construction (architects, lawyers, fees) |
| **Absorption rate** | Speed at which units sell or lease in the local market |
| **TI allowance** | Tenant improvement budget provided by landlord |
| **Development agreement** | Contract with city defining project requirements and fees |

## When to Apply
- Evaluating potential development sites or projects
- Structuring financing for ground-up construction
