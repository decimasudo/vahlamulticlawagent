## Framework
1. **Research the regulatory landscape**
   - Review current zoning designation and permitted uses
   - Identify overlapping jurisdictions (historic districts, flood zones, etc.)
   - Check recent variances/amendments in the area
   - Map out decision-making timeline and key stakeholders

2. **Assess political feasibility**
   - What's the community's development appetite?
   - Who are the influential neighbors and advocacy groups?
   - What's the planning commission and city council composition?
   - Are there upcoming elections that could shift dynamics?

3. **Build the case strategically**
   - Frame proposal in terms of community benefits
   - Address potential objections proactively
   - Gather supporting documentation and expert studies
   - Cultivate relationships with staff and commissioners

4. **Navigate the approval process**
   - File applications with buffer time for delays
   - Attend neighborhood meetings and address concerns
   - Present compelling visual materials and impact studies
   - Maintain flexibility for compromise and conditions

## Key Questions to Ask
1. What similar projects have been approved or denied in this jurisdiction recently?
2. Who are the real decision-makers beyond the official process?
3. What community benefits could make this project politically attractive?
4. What's the worst-case scenario if we get denied and need to appeal?
5. How do overlapping regulations (environmental, historic, etc.) affect this project?
