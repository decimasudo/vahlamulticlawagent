## Framework
1. **Performance Analysis**
   - Track NOI trends and variance from budget
   - Benchmark against market comparables
   - Analyze tenant rollover and renewal patterns
   - Model various exit scenario valuations

2. **Operating Optimization**
   - Implement cost reduction without impacting tenant satisfaction
   - Maximize rental rates through strategic improvements
   - Optimize tenant mix and lease structures
   - Manage vendor relationships and service contracts

3. **Capital Planning**
   - Prioritize capex based on ROI and tenant retention
   - Plan major system replacements and renovations
   - Balance maintenance vs. improvement spending
   - Coordinate improvements with lease expirations

4. **Disposition Strategy**
   - Monitor market conditions and cap rate trends
   - Identify optimal sale timing within hold period
   - Prepare property for maximum sale value
   - Execute tax-efficient exit strategies

## Key Questions to Ask
1. What's driving NOI performance vs. budget and market?
2. Which capital improvements will generate best returns?
3. How do current market conditions affect hold vs. sell decision?
4. Are we maximizing income while maintaining tenant satisfaction?
5. What's our 3-5 year plan for this asset's highest and best use?

## Vocabulary
| Term | Plain English |
|------|---------------|
| **NOI** | Net Operating Income after expenses but before debt service |
| **Capex** | Capital expenditures for improvements lasting more than one year |
| **T12** | Trailing twelve months of financial performance |
| **Market rent** | Current rental rates for comparable space in the area |
| **Stabilization** | Point when property reaches projected occupancy and income |

## When to Apply
- Managing existing real estate investment portfolios
- Analyzing property performance and improvement opportunities
- Planning capital expenditure priorities and timing
- Evaluating hold vs. sell decisions for mature assets
