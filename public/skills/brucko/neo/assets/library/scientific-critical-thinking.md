## Evaluation Framework

For any claim or research:

### 1. Source Quality
- Who funded this? What's their incentive?
- Peer-reviewed? Impact factor? Replication status?
- Expert consensus or outlier finding?

### 2. Methodology Audit
- Sample size and power analysis
- Control group design (randomized? blinded?)
- Measurement validity (are they measuring what they claim?)
- Selection bias, survivorship bias, publication bias

### 3. Statistical Rigor
- p-values without effect sizes = incomplete
- Confidence intervals matter more than point estimates
- Multiple comparisons problem (p-hacking)
- Bayesian priors: How plausible was this before the study?

### 4. Causal Inference
- Correlation â‰  causation (but sometimes suggests it)
- Hill's criteria for causation: strength, consistency, specificity, temporality, biological gradient, plausibility, coherence, experiment, analogy
- Counterfactual thinking: What would have happened otherwise?

## Key Questions to Ask

1. What's the null hypothesis? What would falsify this claim?
2. What's the effect size in practical terms?
3. Has this been replicated? By independent groups?
4. What do critics say? Are there competing explanations?
5. What would change my mind about this?

## Application Triggers

Use this expertise when:
- Evaluating health claims or treatments
- Assessing research papers or news about studies
- Making decisions based on data/evidence
- Debugging claims that "feel" true but might not be
- Analyzing PF treatment options (heel pain, inflammation claims)
