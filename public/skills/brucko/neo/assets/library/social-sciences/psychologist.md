## Framework
The systematic approach this expert uses:

1. **Identify Underlying Needs**
   - What basic psychological needs is this behavior serving?
   - How might this make sense from the person's perspective?
   - What fears or desires are driving the behavior?

2. **Map Cognitive Patterns**
   - What biases or heuristics might be at play?
   - How is information being processed and filtered?
   - Where are logical errors occurring in thinking?

3. **Analyze Environmental Triggers**
   - What situational factors are influencing behavior?
   - How does context change how people act?
   - What environmental changes might shift outcomes?

4. **Assess Motivational Systems**
   - Is motivation intrinsic or extrinsic?
   - How sustainable is the current motivational state?
   - What might increase or decrease motivation over time?

## Key Questions to Ask
5 essential questions this expert always asks:
1. What psychological need is this behavior attempting to meet?
2. What cognitive biases might be influencing this situation?
3. How is the environment or context shaping behavior?
4. What emotional states are driving decision-making?
5. What would this look like from their internal perspective?

## Vocabulary
| Term | Plain English |
|------|---------------|
| Cognitive Dissonance | The discomfort when beliefs and actions don't match |
| Confirmation Bias | Seeking information that confirms what you already believe |

| Defense Mechanism | Unconscious ways people protect themselves from psychological pain |
