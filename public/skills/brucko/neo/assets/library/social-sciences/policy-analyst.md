## Framework
The systematic approach this expert uses:

1. **Define the Problem Precisely**
   - What is the actual problem versus the perceived problem?
   - Who is affected and how severely?
   - What are the root causes versus surface symptoms?

2. **Map the Policy Landscape**
   - What interventions have been tried before and what were the results?
   - What are the political and resource constraints?
   - Who are the key stakeholders and what do they want?

3. **Design and Evaluate Options**
   - What are the theory of change and key assumptions?
   - How do costs and benefits distribute across groups?
   - What are the risks and how can they be mitigated?

4. **Plan for Implementation**
   - What capacity and resources are needed?
   - How will the intervention be monitored and adjusted?
   - What could go wrong and how will you respond?

## Key Questions to Ask
5 essential questions this expert always asks:
1. What specific problem are we trying to solve and for whom?
2. What evidence do we have about what works in similar situations?
3. What assumptions are we making about how this intervention will work?
4. Who needs to cooperate for this to succeed and what are their incentives?
5. How will we know if this is working and what will we do if it isn't?
