## Framework
1. **Client assessment and positioning**
   - Evaluate current market value and identify unique selling propositions
   - Analyze career trajectory and identify gaps or weaknesses to address
   - Position client within appropriate peer group and competitive landscape
   - Develop compelling narrative around client's brand and career evolution

2. **Opportunity identification and pursuit**
   - Monitor casting notices, development pipelines, and industry job boards
   - Leverage relationships with casting directors, producers, and executives
   - Create opportunities through strategic introductions and package deals
   - Anticipate market shifts and position clients for emerging trends

3. **Negotiation and deal-making**
   - Research comparable deals and establish fair market value benchmarks
   - Prioritize deal points that matter most for client's long-term goals
   - Build relationships with business affairs executives at all major companies
   - Structure deals with escalators, bonuses, and backend participation

4. **Career management and strategic planning**
   - Schedule regular strategy sessions to assess goals and market position
   - Coordinate with publicists, managers, and lawyers for holistic representation
   - Plan career moves 2-3 projects ahead to build momentum
   - Manage client expectations while pushing for ambitious opportunities

## Key Questions to Ask
1. What does career success look like for this client in 5 years?
2. How does this opportunity advance their brand and open future doors?
3. What relationships do I need to build to access better opportunities for this client?
4. Are we pricing this client appropriately for their current market value?
5. What's the client's risk tolerance for taking creative or financial chances?

## Vocabulary
| Term | Plain English |
|------|---------------|
| **Packaging** | Assembling multiple clients (actor, director, writer) for single project |
| **Backend** | Profit participation that pays out after production costs are recouped |
| **Quote** | Client's established rate from previous deal, used as negotiation baseline |
| **Pay-or-play** | Guarantee that client gets paid even if project doesn't move forward |
| **Favored nations** | Agreement that client gets same terms as other actors in similar roles |

## When to Apply
- Negotiating contracts, deals, or agreements where relationship dynamics matter
- Building long-term strategic partnerships with mutual benefit potential
- Advocating for others in competitive situations with multiple stakeholders
- Managing careers, projects, or initiatives requiring market positioning
