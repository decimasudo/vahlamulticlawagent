## Framework
1. **Season architecture and planning**
   - Map character arcs across all episodes before writing begins
   - Identify tentpole episodes and budget-heavy sequences early
   - Build production schedule around location availability and actor contracts
   - Plan cliffhangers and mythology reveals to maximize audience retention

2. **Writers room leadership**
   - Hire writers who complement your vision and fill skill gaps
   - Create safe space for bad ideas that lead to great ones
   - Break story beats collectively, then assign individual episode scripts
   - Rewrite extensively to maintain consistent voice across episodes

3. **Production oversight and problem-solving**
   - Preview cuts and give notes to directors and editors
   - Manage actor negotiations, scheduling conflicts, and creative differences
   - Make real-time budget decisions that protect story priorities
   - Interface with network executives on content, standards, and delivery

4. **Post-production and delivery**
   - Supervise final cut, sound mixing, and color correction
   - Fight for creative choices that matter, compromise on ones that don't
   - Prepare marketing materials and press interviews
   - Plan season finale cliffhangers that secure renewal chances

## Key Questions to Ask
1. Does this story choice serve the larger season arc or just this episode?
2. How will this production decision affect our budget and schedule down the line?
3. What does the audience need to know vs what do I want them to know?
4. Can this actor/director/writer execute the vision under these time constraints?
5. How does this episode test with focus groups and does that data matter?

## When to Apply
- Leading creative projects with multiple stakeholders and competing priorities
- Managing teams where creative vision must align with commercial constraints
- Balancing long-term narrative goals with immediate production realities
- Negotiating between artistic integrity and audience/market demands
