## Framework
1. **Project identification and acquisition**
   - Monitor spec scripts, pitch meetings, and international format sales
   - Attend film festivals, theater productions, and book launches for source material
   - Maintain relationships with agencies, management companies, and production companies
   - Track successful creators from other networks who might be available

2. **Creative evaluation and development**
   - Assess script quality, character development, and franchise potential
   - Evaluate attached talent's track record and current market value
   - Estimate production costs and compare to similar successful shows
   - Consider international sales potential and merchandising opportunities

3. **Packaging and deal-making**
   - Match projects with appropriate showrunners and executive producers
   - Negotiate talent deals, production contracts, and profit participation
   - Structure development timelines and milestone payments
   - Coordinate legal, business affairs, and standards & practices reviews

4. **Greenlight process and launch**
   - Present projects to senior executives with audience research and comps
   - Defend creative choices while incorporating network feedback
   - Manage pilot production and testing process
   - Plan marketing strategy and launch timing for maximum impact

## Key Questions to Ask
1. What makes this story uniquely suited for our network's brand and audience?
2. Can this premise sustain 100+ episodes or is it better as limited series?
3. How does this project's budget compare to ratings expectations and ad revenue potential?
4. What's our fallback plan if this showrunner leaves after season two?
5. How will this show perform in international sales and streaming markets?

## Vocabulary
| Term | Plain English |
|------|---------------|
| **Format** | Show structure that can be adapted across different countries/cultures |
| **First-look deal** | Agreement giving network first rights to creator's future projects |
| **Pilot season** | Traditional spring period when networks order and test new shows |
| **Deficit financing** | Studio pays production costs above network license fee, betting on backend profits |
| **Upfront commitment** | Network orders multiple episodes before seeing pilot results |

## When to Apply
- Evaluating creative projects or business opportunities with uncertain commercial potential
- Building relationships with talent and creators for long-term strategic advantage
- Making content acquisition decisions under budget constraints and competitive pressure
- Balancing artistic vision with market research and audience data
