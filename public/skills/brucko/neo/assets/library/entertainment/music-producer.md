## Framework
1. **Pre-production planning**
   - Listen to artist's demos and identify strongest songs for development
   - Arrange songs to highlight artist's strengths and mask weaknesses
   - Plan instrumentation, key changes, and overall sonic palette
   - Book appropriate studios, musicians, and engineers for the vision

2. **Recording and performance direction**
   - Create comfortable environment where artist can deliver authentic performances
   - Use psychological techniques to overcome recording anxiety and perfectionism
   - Layer instruments and vocals to create depth without cluttering the mix
   - Capture happy accidents and spontaneous moments that add magic

3. **Sound design and mixing**
   - Choose microphones, preamps, and processing to match song's emotional tone
   - Build mix from foundation up: drums, bass, then melodic and harmonic elements
   - Use spatial effects and automation to create movement and interest
   - Master tracks for optimal playback across different systems and platforms

4. **Artist development and creative guidance**
   - Help artists find their unique voice and artistic identity
   - Push creative boundaries while maintaining commercial viability
   - Develop artists' technical skills and studio confidence over time
   - Build long-term relationships that evolve across multiple projects

## Key Questions to Ask
1. What emotion is this song trying to convey and does every element support that?
2. How can we make this artist sound unique in an oversaturated market?
3. What's the one thing that makes this song memorable after first listen?
4. Are we serving the song or showing off our production skills?
5. How will this track sound on phone speakers, car stereos, and headphones?

## Vocabulary
| Term | Plain English |
|------|---------------|
| **Comping** | Combining best parts from multiple takes into single perfect performance |
| **Bus processing** | Applying effects to groups of tracks rather than individual elements |
| **Reference track** | Commercial song used as mixing and mastering benchmark |
| **Top line** | Vocal melody and lyrics written over existing instrumental track |
| **A&R** | Artist and Repertoire; record label executive who finds and develops talent |

## When to Apply
- Leading creative projects requiring both technical skill and artistic vision
- Drawing peak performance from team members in high-pressure situations
- Balancing creative authenticity with commercial market requirements
- Managing complex projects with multiple contributors and competing visions
