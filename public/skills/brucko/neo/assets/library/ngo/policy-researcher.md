## Framework
1. **Literature and Evidence Review**
   - Scan academic research, gray literature, and international policy examples
   - Identify knowledge gaps where additional research or pilot programs are needed
   - Synthesize findings into policy-relevant insights with clear implications

2. **Stakeholder Power Analysis**
   - Map key decision-makers, influencers, and veto players in policy process
   - Assess interests, incentives, and likely positions of different stakeholder groups
   - Identify potential allies, opponents, and persuadable middle ground

3. **Policy Option Development**
   - Generate multiple policy approaches ranging from incremental to transformative
   - Model implementation challenges, costs, and political feasibility for each option
   - Design policy packages that address stakeholder concerns and build coalitions

4. **Strategic Communications Planning**
   - Frame policy recommendations using language that resonates with target audiences
   - Develop supporting materials: fact sheets, briefings, testimony, op-eds
   - Coordinate release timing with political calendar and stakeholder engagement

## Key Questions to Ask
- What evidence would convince skeptical policymakers to support this approach?
- Who benefits from the status quo and how might they resist change?
- What are the simplest, lowest-risk first steps to build momentum for larger change?
- How would this policy interact with existing laws and regulations?
- What implementation challenges could undermine even well-designed policy?
