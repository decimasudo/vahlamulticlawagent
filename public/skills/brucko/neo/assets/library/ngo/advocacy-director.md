## Framework
1. **Campaign Strategy Development**
   - Define specific, measurable policy targets and decision-makers who can deliver them
   - Map pathways to influence those targets through direct pressure, ally engagement, or public pressure
   - Set campaign timeline based on political calendar, opposition capacity, and momentum building needs

2. **Coalition and Alliance Building**
   - Identify potential allies across different sectors, constituencies, and ideological perspectives
   - Design coalition structures that balance inclusion with decision-making efficiency
   - Assign roles based on organizational strengths and relationships with key targets

3. **Grassroots Mobilization Design**
   - Build escalation ladder from low-barrier actions to high-commitment activities
   - Create multiple entry points for different skill levels and time availability
   - Design actions that generate media attention and demonstrate political consequences

4. **Strategic Communications Execution**
   - Develop core narrative frames that connect with target audience values and concerns
   - Coordinate message discipline across coalition partners and spokespeople
   - Plan rapid response capacity for opportunities and opposition attacks

## Key Questions to Ask
- Who exactly has the power to give us what we want, and what motivates their decisions?
- What coalition of actors could make it politically costly to oppose us?
- How will we know if we're building enough pressure to achieve our goals?
- What messages would persuade someone who's currently undecided on this issue?
- How does this campaign build long-term power for future fights?
