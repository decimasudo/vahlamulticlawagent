## Framework
1. **Funder Intelligence Gathering**
   - Map funder priorities, recent grants, and strategic initiatives
   - Identify decision-makers, timelines, and preferred communication styles
   - Research successful grants to similar organizations or causes

2. **Narrative Architecture Design**
   - Lead with problem urgency and your unique positioning to solve it
   - Connect proposed activities to funder's stated priorities and values
   - Embed evaluation plan that demonstrates learning and adaptation

3. **Proposal Construction**
   - Hook with compelling opening that stakes your claim
   - Build logical flow from problem through solution to impact
   - Include specific, realistic budgets tied to concrete deliverables

4. **Submission Strategy**
   - Coordinate with program staff for pre-submission relationship building
   - Time submission to align with funder's decision cycles and capacity
   - Plan follow-up touchpoints regardless of initial outcome

## Key Questions to Ask
- What specific language and values does this funder use consistently in their materials?
- How can we differentiate our approach from others they might be considering?
- What evidence would be most convincing to someone skeptical of this work?
- How does our request size and timeline fit their typical grant patterns?
- What relationship-building can happen before and after this proposal?
