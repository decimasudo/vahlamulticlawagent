## Framework
1. **Data collection and validation**
   - Gather relevant historical data with sufficient sample size
   - Clean data for outliers, missing values, and reporting errors
   - Test for statistical biases and selection effects
   - Establish baseline assumptions and confidence intervals

2. **Model development and testing**
   - Build predictive models using appropriate statistical techniques
   - Validate models against holdout data and historical back-testing
   - Stress-test assumptions under extreme scenarios
   - Document methodology and limitations clearly

3. **Risk quantification and pricing**
   - Calculate expected values and tail risk scenarios
   - Build in safety margins for model uncertainty
   - Price products to achieve target profit margins after expenses
   - Consider regulatory capital requirements and solvency ratios

4. **Monitoring and adjustment**
   - Track actual vs expected experience regularly
   - Update assumptions based on emerging data trends
   - Recalibrate models when significant deviations occur
   - Communicate findings to stakeholders with appropriate caveats

## Key Questions to Ask
1. What's the confidence interval around this estimate and why should I trust it?
2. How sensitive are the results to changes in key assumptions?
3. What's the worst-case scenario and can the organization survive it?
4. Are there regulatory or external factors that could invalidate this model?
5. How does this risk correlate with other exposures in the portfolio?

## Vocabulary
| Term | Plain English |
|------|---------------|
| **Mortality table** | Statistical chart showing death rates by age, gender, and other factors |
| **Loss ratio** | Claims paid divided by premiums collected (insurance profitability measure) |
| **Reserves** | Money set aside to pay future claims on existing policies |
| **Credibility** | Statistical reliability of data based on sample size and variance |
| **Tail risk** | Low-probability, high-impact events that can destroy capital |

## When to Apply
- Evaluating insurance products, warranties, or long-term contracts
- Analyzing safety margins and worst-case scenarios in financial planning
- Making decisions involving statistical uncertainty and incomplete data
- Assessing demographic trends and their long-term implications
