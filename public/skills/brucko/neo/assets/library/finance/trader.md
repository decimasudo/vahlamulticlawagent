## Framework
1. **Pre-market preparation**
   - Review overnight flows and international market moves
   - Identify key levels, support/resistance, and volatility expectations
   - Set position size limits and risk budgets for the day
   - Plan entry/exit scenarios before markets open

2. **Trade setup and execution**
   - Wait for high-probability setups that match your edge
   - Use limit orders to control fills and slippage
   - Scale into positions rather than going all-in at once
   - Monitor order flow and level 2 data for execution timing

3. **Active position management**
   - Trail stops as positions move in your favor
   - Scale out at predetermined profit targets
   - Cut losers quickly when thesis is invalidated
   - Hedge correlated exposures to reduce portfolio risk

4. **Post-trade analysis**
   - Review every trade for execution quality and timing
   - Calculate risk-adjusted returns and Sharpe ratios
   - Identify patterns in winning vs losing trades
   - Adjust position sizing based on recent performance

## Key Questions to Ask
1. What's my maximum loss if this trade goes completely against me?
2. What's the market microstructure telling me about supply/demand imbalance?
3. Am I trading my plan or letting emotions drive decisions?
4. What's my win rate and average win/loss ratio over the last 100 trades?
5. How does this position fit into my overall portfolio risk profile?

## Vocabulary
| Term | Plain English |
|------|---------------|
| **Slippage** | The difference between expected and actual execution price |
| **Edge** | Statistical advantage that makes your strategy profitable over time |
| **Drawdown** | Peak-to-trough decline in account value during losing period |
| **Beta** | How much your position moves relative to the broader market |
| **Greeks** | Risk sensitivities (delta, gamma, theta, vega) for options positions |

## When to Apply
- Evaluating investment opportunities with quantifiable risk/reward
- Managing portfolios or positions with multiple moving parts
- Making decisions under uncertainty with incomplete information
- Optimizing execution timing for large transactions or purchases
