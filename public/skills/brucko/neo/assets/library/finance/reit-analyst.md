## Framework
1. **Fundamental Analysis**
   - Calculate and trend FFO/AFFO per share growth
   - Analyze same-store NOI growth by property type
   - Evaluate occupancy trends and lease rollover schedules
   - Assess management's track record and strategic vision

2. **Valuation Methods**
   - Apply multiple NAV approaches using cap rates
   - Calculate P/FFO and P/AFFO multiples vs. peers
   - Model dividend yield sustainability and coverage
   - Analyze total return potential across time horizons

3. **Capital Structure Review**
   - Evaluate debt-to-equity ratios and coverage metrics
   - Analyze debt maturity schedule and refinancing risk
   - Assess access to capital markets and cost of funds
   - Review development pipeline funding requirements

4. **Sector & Market Positioning**
   - Compare portfolio quality and geographic diversification
   - Analyze exposure to growth vs. value markets
   - Evaluate competitive positioning and market share
   - Assess external growth strategy and acquisition pipeline

## Key Questions to Ask
1. How sustainable is current FFO/AFFO growth given market conditions?
2. What's the true quality of this REIT's portfolio vs. reported metrics?
3. How vulnerable is this REIT to interest rate changes?
4. Can management execute their stated strategy with current capital structure?
5. How does current valuation compare to private market transactions?

## Vocabulary
| Term | Plain English |
|------|---------------|
| **FFO** | Funds From Operations, REIT equivalent of earnings excluding depreciation |
| **AFFO** | Adjusted FFO after subtracting recurring capex and leasing costs |
| **NAV** | Net Asset Value based on individual property valuations |
| **Same-store NOI** | Income growth from existing properties, excluding acquisitions |
| **Payout ratio** | Percentage of FFO/AFFO paid out as dividends |

## When to Apply
- Analyzing REIT investments for purchase or sale decisions
- Evaluating REIT sector rotation and relative value opportunities
- Modeling REIT dividend sustainability and total return potential
- Comparing public REIT valuations to private real estate markets
