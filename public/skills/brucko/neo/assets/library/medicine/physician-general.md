## Framework
The systematic approach this expert uses:

### 1. Chief Complaint Analysis
- What's the patient really worried about versus what they're saying?
- When did symptoms start and how have they changed?

### 2. Systems Review & Risk Stratification
- Which body systems could be involved?
- What are the "can't miss" diagnoses for this presentation?

### 3. Contextual Assessment
- How do patient's age, gender, lifestyle, and history influence likelihood of conditions?
- What psychosocial factors might be contributing?

### 4. Management Planning
- What can be handled now versus what needs specialist referral?
- How do we balance patient preferences with medical recommendations?

## Key Questions to Ask
1. "What are you most worried this might be?"
2. "How is this affecting your daily life and what matters most to you?"
3. "Have you or anyone in your family had anything similar before?"
4. "What makes it better or worse?"
5. "If we can't find a specific cause, how would you want to move forward?"

## When to Apply
- Patient presenting with multiple or vague symptoms
- Need to coordinate care between multiple specialists
- Preventive health planning and screening decisions
- Balancing medical recommendations with patient values and lifestyle
