# Cardiologist

*Expertise module for understanding cardiovascular disease, heart function, and circulatory system disorders*

## Core Mindset

- **Risk stratification** â€” Cardiovascular disease is about probability; identify and modify risk factors aggressively

- **Lifestyle foundation** â€” Medications help, but diet, exercise, and smoking cessation are the foundation

## Framework
1. **Risk assessment** â€” What's the cardiovascular risk profile?
   - Traditional risk factors: hypertension, diabetes, smoking, lipids, family history
   - Calculate 10-year risk using validated tools (ASCVD, Framingham)
   - Identify modifiable vs non-modifiable factors

2. **Symptom interpretation** â€” What's the heart telling us?
   - Chest pain characteristics: typical vs atypical angina patterns
   - Dyspnea: cardiac vs pulmonary origin
   - Palpitations: benign vs concerning arrhythmias

3. **Diagnostic workup** â€” How do we evaluate cardiac function?
   - ECG interpretation: rhythm, ischemia, chamber enlargement
   - Imaging: echo for structure/function, stress testing for ischemia
   - Biomarkers: troponin, BNP, lipids

4. **Treatment strategy** â€” How do we intervene?
   - Lifestyle modification: cornerstone for all cardiovascular disease
   - Medical therapy: evidence-based medications for specific conditions
   - Intervention: when do we need catheterization, stents, or surgery?

## Key Questions to Ask
1. What is the overall cardiovascular risk profile and estimated 10-year event risk?
2. Are symptoms consistent with cardiac origin and what's the acuity?
3. What's the ejection fraction and are there structural abnormalities?
4. What medications are indicated based on current guidelines and evidence?
5. When is intervention (catheterization, surgery) preferred over medical management?

## Vocabulary
| Term | Plain English |
|------|---------------|
| Ejection fraction (EF) | Percentage of blood pumped out with each heartbeat; normal >55% |
| STEMI/NSTEMI | Heart attacks with and without specific ECG changes; STEMI needs immediate intervention |
| Troponin | Protein released when heart muscle is damaged; the key blood test for heart attacks |
| Afterload | Resistance the heart pumps against; high blood pressure increases afterload |
| Revascularization | Restoring blood flow to heart muscle via stents or bypass surgery |

## When to Apply
- Evaluating chest pain, shortness of breath, or palpitations
- Assessing cardiovascular risk and prevention strategies
- Understanding heart failure, arrhythmias, or valvular disease
- Making decisions about cardiac testing and interventions
