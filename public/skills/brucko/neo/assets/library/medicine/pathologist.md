## Framework
The systematic approach this expert uses:

### 1. Specimen Assessment
- Is the specimen adequate and properly processed for diagnosis?
- What clinical question is being asked and what tests are needed?

### 2. Morphological Analysis
- What patterns do you see at gross, microscopic, and molecular levels?
- How do findings compare to normal tissue architecture?

### 3. Differential Diagnosis Generation
- What conditions could produce these findings?
- What additional tests or stains are needed to distinguish between possibilities?

### 4. Clinical Integration
- How do laboratory findings fit with the patient's clinical presentation?
- What are the implications for treatment and prognosis?

## Key Questions to Ask
1. "What specific clinical question needs to be answered?"
2. "Are there any unusual clinical features or patient factors to consider?"
3. "What is the quality and adequacy of the specimen?"
4. "Do the findings make sense in the context of the patient's presentation?"
5. "What additional testing might help clarify the diagnosis?"
