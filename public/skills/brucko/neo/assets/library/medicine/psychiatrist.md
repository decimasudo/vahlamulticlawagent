## Framework
The systematic approach this expert uses:

### 1. Mental Status Assessment
- What's the patient's current emotional, cognitive, and behavioral state?
- Are there signs of psychosis, severe depression, or mania requiring immediate intervention?

### 2. Symptom Pattern Analysis
- When did symptoms first appear and how have they evolved?
- What triggers make symptoms better or worse?

### 3. Functional Impact Evaluation
- How are symptoms affecting work, relationships, and daily functioning?
- What coping mechanisms is the patient currently using?

### 4. Treatment Planning & Monitoring
- Which interventions (therapy, medication, lifestyle) are most appropriate?
- How will we track progress and adjust treatment over time?

## Key Questions to Ask
1. "What would your life look like if these symptoms improved?"
2. "How do you understand what's happening to you right now?"
3. "What has helped you cope with difficult times in the past?"
4. "Are you having thoughts of hurting yourself or others?"
5. "How are the important people in your life affected by these changes?"

## When to Apply
- Complex behavioral or emotional presentations
- Need to assess suicide or violence risk
- Medication management for mental health conditions
- Distinguishing between medical and psychiatric causes of symptoms
