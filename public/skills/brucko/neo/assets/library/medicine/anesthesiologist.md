# Anesthesiologist

*Expertise module for perioperative care, pain management, and maintaining patient stability during procedures*

## Core Mindset

## Framework
1. **Preoperative assessment** â€” Who is this patient?
   - Airway evaluation: Mallampati, neck mobility, mouth opening, history of difficult intubation
   - Comorbidities: cardiac, pulmonary, renal, hepatic function
   - Medications and allergies; NPO status
   - Anesthetic history and family history (malignant hyperthermia)

2. **Anesthetic planning** â€” What's the best approach?
   - General vs regional vs monitored anesthesia care
   - Induction agents, maintenance strategy, muscle relaxation needs
   - Monitoring requirements based on patient and procedure
   - Contingency plans: difficult airway, massive hemorrhage, cardiac events

3. **Intraoperative management** â€” Maintain stability
   - Continuous monitoring: ECG, SpO2, ETCO2, BP, temperature
   - Fluid and blood product management
   - Depth of anesthesia: too light = awareness, too deep = hemodynamic collapse
   - Anticipate surgical events and adjust proactively

4. **Emergence and recovery** â€” Safe transition
   - Reversal of neuromuscular blockade
   - Extubation criteria: awake, adequate respiratory effort, protective reflexes
   - Postoperative pain control and PONV prophylaxis
   - Handoff to recovery team with complete information

## Key Questions to Ask
1. What are the patient's airway characteristics and is difficult intubation anticipated?
2. What are the relevant comorbidities and how will they affect anesthetic management?
3. What is the optimal anesthetic technique for this patient and procedure?
4. What complications are most likely, and am I prepared to manage them?
5. What is the pain management plan for postoperative recovery?

## Vocabulary
| Term | Plain English |
|------|---------------|
| Mallampati score | Airway assessment based on visible structures with mouth open; higher = harder intubation |
| MAC | Monitored Anesthesia Care (sedation without full general anesthesia); or Minimum Alveolar Concentration of inhaled anesthetics |
| Neuromuscular blockade | Paralysis using drugs that block the nerve-muscle junction; must be reversed |
| ETCO2 | End-tidal CO2; exhaled carbon dioxide level monitored breath-by-breath |
| ASA classification | Physical status classification (I-VI) predicting perioperative risk |

## When to Apply
- Evaluating patients for surgery and perioperative risk
- Understanding sedation and pain management options
- Recognizing and responding to acute physiologic emergencies
- Discussing anesthetic risks and planning for procedures
