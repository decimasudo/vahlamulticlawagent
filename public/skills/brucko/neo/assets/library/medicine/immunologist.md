*Expertise module for understanding immune system function, allergic diseases, and immunodeficiency disorders*

## Framework
1. **Pattern recognition** â€” What type of immune problem?
   - Immunodeficiency: recurrent, severe, unusual, or difficult-to-treat infections
   - Allergy/hypersensitivity: IgE-mediated (type I), immune complex, or cell-mediated reactions
   - Autoimmunity: immune attack on self-tissues; organ-specific or systemic

2. **History taking** â€” The diagnostic goldmine
   - Infection history: type, frequency, location, pathogens, response to treatment
   - Reaction history: triggers, timing (immediate vs delayed), symptoms, reproducibility
   - Family history: immunodeficiency, autoimmunity, atopy
   - Medications and treatments: immunosuppressants, biologics, prior immunotherapy

3. **Laboratory evaluation** â€” Targeted immune workup
   - Quantitative immunoglobulins: IgG, IgA, IgM, IgE
   - Lymphocyte subsets: T cells, B cells, NK cells
   - Specific antibody responses: vaccine titers
   - Specialized testing: complement, neutrophil function, genetic testing

4. **Management strategy** â€” Support, replace, or modulate
   - Immunodeficiency: prophylactic antibiotics, immunoglobulin replacement, stem cell transplant
   - Allergy: avoidance, antihistamines, immunotherapy, biologics for severe disease
   - Autoimmunity: immunosuppression targeted to the mechanism

## Key Questions to Ask
1. What is the pattern of infections (site, pathogen, frequency, severity)?
2. What was the exact timing between exposure and reaction (minutes = IgE; days = delayed)?
3. What is the family history of immune problems, allergies, or autoimmunity?
4. What vaccines has the patient received and did they respond appropriately?
5. Are there signs of autoimmunity affecting other organ systems?

## Vocabulary
| Term | Plain English |
|------|---------------|
| Atopy | Genetic tendency toward allergic diseases (eczema, asthma, allergic rhinitis) |
| Anaphylaxis | Severe, potentially life-threatening allergic reaction affecting multiple body systems |
| Immunoglobulin | Antibody proteins (IgG, IgA, IgM, IgE) that fight infections or cause allergic reactions |
| T cells/B cells | Key immune cells; T cells coordinate response and kill infected cells; B cells make antibodies |
| Immunotherapy | Treatment that modifies immune response; in allergy, builds tolerance to triggers over time |

## When to Apply
- Evaluating recurrent or unusual infections
- Working up suspected allergic reactions or anaphylaxis
- Understanding autoimmune conditions and their treatment
- Assessing immune status before immunosuppressive therapy
