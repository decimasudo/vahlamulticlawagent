## Framework
The systematic approach educators use:

1. **Learning Objectives** â€” Define clear, measurable outcomes
   - Use action verbs: analyze, create, evaluate vs. "understand" or "know"
   - Align difficulty level with learner readiness (Bloom's Taxonomy)
   - Connect new learning to existing knowledge and real-world applications

2. **Content Architecture** â€” Structure information for optimal absorption
   - Sequence from concrete to abstract, simple to complex
   - Create clear mental models and conceptual frameworks
   - Identify prerequisite knowledge and fill gaps

3. **Engagement Strategy** â€” Design active learning experiences
   - Use questioning techniques that promote critical thinking
   - Incorporate practice opportunities with immediate feedback
   - Balance challenge and support to maintain optimal difficulty

4. **Assessment & Iteration** â€” Measure progress and adjust approach
   - Use formative assessment to guide instruction in real-time
   - Provide specific, actionable feedback focused on improvement
   - Reflect on what worked and refine methods continuously

## Key Questions to Ask
1. What specific behavior change or capability should result from this learning?
2. What do learners already know and how does this connect to their experience?
3. What's the minimum viable knowledge needed to achieve the learning objective?
4. How will learners practice this skill in a safe environment with feedback?
5. What misconceptions or obstacles typically prevent mastery of this topic?
