## Framework
The systematic approach negotiators use:

1. **Preparation Phase** â€” Research and strategize before engagement
   - Map all parties' interests, constraints, and decision-making processes
   - Identify your BATNA (Best Alternative to Negotiated Agreement) and estimate theirs
   - Plan opening moves, concession strategy, and walk-away triggers

2. **Information Gathering** â€” Learn through strategic questioning and listening
   - Use open-ended questions to understand underlying interests
   - Listen for what they're not saying; watch for non-verbal cues
   - Test assumptions and probe for flexibility in their positions

3. **Value Creation** â€” Find mutually beneficial solutions
   - Identify differences in priorities, timing, risk tolerance, or resources
   - Brainstorm options before evaluating them (separate creation from claiming)
   - Package issues together to create trade-offs across different variables

4. **Agreement Structuring** â€” Lock in deals that stick
   - Address implementation details and contingency scenarios
   - Ensure all parties understand commitments and consequences
   - Plan communication and relationship maintenance going forward

## Key Questions to Ask
1. What does success look like for each party involved?
2. What would have to be true for them to say yes to this proposal?
3. What are the consequences if we don't reach an agreement?
4. Where do our interests align and where do they diverge?
5. What creative solutions could satisfy both parties' core needs?

## Vocabulary
| Term | Plain English |
|------|---------------|
| **BATNA** | Best Alternative to Negotiated Agreement - your fallback option |
| **Anchoring** | Setting reference point that influences subsequent discussions |
| **Zone of Possible Agreement (ZOPA)** | Range where a deal satisfies both parties' minimum requirements |
| **Integrative vs. Distributive** | Win-win value creation vs. zero-sum claiming |
| **Reservation Price** | Minimum acceptable outcome; your walk-away point |
