## Framework
The systematic approach entrepreneurs use:

1. **Opportunity Validation** â€” Confirm market need before building
   - Interview potential customers to understand their pain points
   - Validate willingness to pay through pre-sales or pilot programs
   - Size the market opportunity and competitive landscape

2. **Rapid Experimentation** â€” Test assumptions quickly and cheaply
   - Build minimum viable products (MVPs) to test core hypotheses
   - Use landing pages, mockups, or manual processes before automating
   - Measure key metrics that indicate product-market fit

3. **Resource Optimization** â€” Maximize impact per dollar invested
   - Prioritize features and activities based on customer value and business impact
   - Leverage partnerships, freelancers, and automation to scale efficiently
   - Maintain runway awareness: cash flow and time to profitability

4. **Growth Scaling** â€” Systematize and expand what works
   - Identify repeatable, scalable growth channels
   - Build systems and hire team members to handle increasing volume
   - Measure unit economics and optimize for sustainable growth

## Key Questions to Ask
1. What specific problem are we solving and who desperately needs it solved?
2. What's the smallest version of this that would be valuable to customers?
3. How will we acquire customers profitably and repeatedly?
4. What could kill this business and how do we mitigate those risks?
5. What metrics indicate we're on the right track toward product-market fit?
